Stress Free Exam

A redesign of Inspera to reduce stress during exams

Discover

The problem

We started by forming an overview over how Inspera is used today. The problem we wanted to solve was to make Inspera available to subjects.

Interview

We interviewed various users of digital exam about their experience with Inspera. We then made contact with the project manager for NTNU's collaboration with Inspera as a digital examination platform. From this meeting we gained more knowledge about the process around the set-up. They focused more on the professors use of Inspera and little on the students, and therefor we wanted to put more focus on this user group.

Define

Personas

Based on the initial interviews, we received information about the various users. We used this to make several personas that we could use as a starting point for a new and and better problem statement.

Focus group

From the focus group we arrived at these requirements to make an improved solution:

  • Clear presentation
  • Confirmation of delivery
  • Good overview
  • Drop everything unnecessary
  • More colors
  • Marking of tasks

    • Develop

    New porblem statement

    We narrowed down the focus to delivery of the exam, and arrived at a new problem statement:

    "Eliminate doubt as much as possible and uncertainty during delivery of examination and hinder unnecessary stressors"

    Prototyping

    We tried to implement the requirements we got from the focus group as much as possible. The result was an improved delivery process. In our prototype we include a lock-in to increase security. We have removed the excess information from the delivery side and made the delivery button more prominent. When you press the delivery button you will get a clear and positive confirmation that the exam has been delivered.

    Deliver

    User testing

    We performed a user test on five people where we were confirmed that the delivery process had become clearer. At the same time, we discovered problems with various buttons, among other things the symbol used on the summary button.

    Implementing

    The next step in the project would be to carry out several iterations with prototyping and user testing. Gradually we would contact the project manager for Inspera at NTNU again to suggest improvements to implemented in the system.
