Digital Tools in School

A website for teacher to learn more about digital tools in school

Project description

"Most teachers think that digital tools are good, but the problem is that not everyone knows how to use them.‚Äù

As it is today, teachers have to seek out digital tools themselves, and they often end up using those they already know. The teachers are often not aware of all the different ways they can use a digital tool, and therefore, they do not get to make full use of its potential.

It is also difficult to get an overview of available digital tools and it can be difficult to find information about how they are used for teaching. So, the problem is that although most teachers think that digital tools are good, not everyone knows how to use them.

Research

Through interviews with teachers, we found that digital tools are a good way to organize teaching and tasks for each individual student. It will also be less visible to the other students which of them will get accommodated tasks, which can take away some of the negative attention this brings.

Digital tools in school can provide many different opportunities in teaching, but teachers do not always have the knowledge to use them. Little information can lead to the digital tools not being used optimally, or not used at all. It can also lead to the teacher spending unnecessary amount of time finding information. That they spend a lot of time finding this information should not be necessary, and teachers could always have more time for preparation.

Based on this, I concluded with two important elements:

  • Time
  • Information

    • Process

    When I was going to start on a solution, it was important to provide knowledge about digital tools by collecting information in one place. It then becomes easier for the teacher to use digital tools as they do not have to look for this information themselves, and they save time.

    After designing a proposal for a website, I user-tested this and received positive feedback on if it was useful and something they would use. I also received feedback on some features that should be added, and after a few iterations i arrived at the solution.

    The solution

    The solution saves teachers time, it gives teachers easy access to information about digital tools, and it allowing them to discover new tools they would not have found if they did not see them on this website.

    More time to spare, more digital tools, and better use of them, can then lead to better organization and planning of the teaching of each individual student.



    Main page

    The solution to the problem was a website where all the digital the tools used in the school are collected. When you enter the website, all the digital tools appear with a logo and a short descriptive title, instead of name, so that even those who have not used the tool before knows what it can be used for (E.g. "Have a quiz" instead of "Kahoot!").

    You can also sort them by category. So if a teacher is looking after a communication tool they can press "Communication" at the top. Other categories can, for example be "Games", "Tasks", "Creative", "Present", etc.

    I made this prototype for PC, so when you hover over a box with a digital tool, you will get three choices. (You can change the way this menu work if the website is to be used on a tablet or a mobile phone).

    Page for one tool

    The first choice in the tool menu is "To website". If you press this, the website to the digital tool will open in a new tab.

    The second choice is "How to use". Here you are taken to a new page that explains in a simple way, step by step and with video, how the digital tool is used. In addition, you get a concrete example you can follow to learn it in practice.

    Q&A

    The last choice is "Q&A". This is another subpage where you get up a list of the most common questions related to this digital tool. When you click on a question, the answer appears directly below.
